OPP GLJRP Niagara Detachment is a structured roleplay community modelled on the real Ontario Provincial Police. As a member, you operate within a defined rank structure, follow standard operating procedures, and represent the detachment with the same discipline and professionalism expected of an actual police service. This documentation is your primary reference for everything from your first day in the Discord to active patrol operations.

## What this documentation covers

This site provides the procedures, standards, and reference material you need to participate effectively in the detachment. Whether you are completing FTO training, writing your first report, or conducting an independent patrol, the relevant guidance is here.

## What to expect as a member

Joining the detachment means committing to a consistent standard of conduct both in-character and when communicating with other members. You will receive a callsign, review the relevant SOPs, complete FTO training alongside an experienced member, and then begin supervised patrol before operating independently.

The documentation here is not optional background reading — it is the operational foundation of the detachment. Procedures exist so that every member behaves predictably and professionally in the field, which makes coordination, report writing, and escalation handling reliable for everyone.

  If you are brand new, go directly to the [new member checklist](/new-member-checklist) before reading anything else. It will direct you to the relevant sections of this documentation in the right order.
</Info>

## Core values

Every procedure and expectation in this detachment flows from four values. Understanding them will help you apply the SOPs correctly even in situations not explicitly covered by a written procedure.

### Professional roleplay

You are representing a police service. In-character conduct should reflect the seriousness of that role — correct use of ranks, appropriate language on radio, and behaviour consistent with an operational officer. Immersion and consistency matter because they make the experience better for every member of the detachment.

### Consistent procedures

The SOPs in this documentation exist because consistency produces predictability. When every member handles a traffic stop, a radio transmission, or a report the same way, supervisors can trust the output and new members can learn by observation. Deviating from procedure without authorisation undermines that reliability.

### Clear communication

Radio discipline and clear communication are non-negotiable. You are expected to use correct radio codes, identify yourself by callsign, and keep transmissions concise. The same standard applies in written communication — reports, Discord messages, and any other correspondence should be unambiguous and professional.

### Proper report writing

Reports are the permanent record of your patrols and incidents. A well-written report protects you, informs supervisors, and provides accurate data for the detachment. The [report writing SOP](/procedures/report-writing) covers the required format in detail, but the underlying principle is simple: write clearly, include everything relevant, and omit everything that is not.
