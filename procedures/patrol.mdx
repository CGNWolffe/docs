A patrol is the core activity of the Niagara Detachment. How you conduct yourself from the moment you check in to the moment you sign off defines the quality of the detachment's roleplay. Follow these procedures on every shift, without exception.

## Beginning a patrol

Before you enter service, complete the following steps in order. Skipping any step creates gaps in accountability and may cause communication issues during the shift.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Join the duty channel">
    Connect to the appropriate voice channel in Discord for your shift. Confirm that your audio input and output are working before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Perform a radio check">
    Transmit a radio check to dispatch or any available unit. Use your callsign and wait for confirmation before continuing.

    > *"Niagara dispatch, [Callsign], radio check, how do you copy, over?"*

    Do not proceed to active patrol until you receive a response. If no response is received after two attempts, report the issue in the duty channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your callsign">
    State your callsign to dispatch and confirm your assigned unit number and patrol area. This creates a record of who is on duty and where they are operating.

    > *"Niagara dispatch, [Callsign] is 10-8 from [location], assigned to [patrol area]."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Acknowledge current advisories">
    Review any active BOLOs, area advisories, or shift briefing notes posted in the Discord duty channel before you begin patrolling.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Your callsign is assigned during onboarding. If you are unsure of your callsign, check the pinned messages in the duty channel or contact a supervisor before going 10-8.
</Note>

## Traffic stops

Traffic stops are the most common contact you will have with civilians during patrol. Approach every stop methodically and professionally. Improvising the approach of a traffic stop is one of the most common sources of safety issues and unrealistic roleplay.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the violation and call in the stop">
    Before activating your lights, mentally note the reason for the stop. As soon as the vehicle pulls over, call the stop into dispatch immediately.

    > *"Niagara dispatch, [Callsign], traffic stop, [location], [vehicle description], [plate if visible]."*

    Wait for dispatch to acknowledge before approaching the vehicle.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approach safely">
    Approach from the driver's rear quarter. Stay aware of traffic and the driver's behaviour before you reach the window. In higher-risk situations, consider a passenger-side approach.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make contact and state the reason">
    Greet the driver, identify yourself, and state the reason for the stop clearly and professionally.

    > *"Good evening, Ontario Provincial Police. The reason I stopped you tonight is [violation]. May I see your licence, registration, and insurance, please?"*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Process and document">
    While the driver provides documents, call in the plate and licence information to dispatch if applicable. Record the interaction details for your report.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Conclude the stop">
    Issue any required warnings, charges, or notices. Advise the driver they are free to go, return to your vehicle, and clear the stop with dispatch.

    > *"Niagara dispatch, [Callsign], clear on the traffic stop at [location]."*
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Never approach a stopped vehicle before calling the stop into dispatch. If a stop escalates to a pursuit or arrest, dispatch must already have your location and vehicle description on record.
</Warning>

## Responding to calls

When dispatch assigns you to a call, your response must match the priority level of the incident. Using an inappropriate response code — either too aggressive or too slow — is a procedural violation.

### Priority levels

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Priority 1 — Emergency response">
    Life-threatening situations requiring an immediate response with lights and sirens. Examples include officer in distress, shots fired, serious injury collisions, or in-progress violent crimes.

    **Response code:** Code 3 (lights and sirens active). Drive as fast as conditions safely allow. Notify dispatch of your ETA and update en route if your route changes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Priority 2 — Urgent response">
    Situations requiring prompt response but without active immediate danger. Examples include disturbances, theft in progress (non-violent), or suspicious person calls.

    **Response code:** Code 2 (expedited, no lights or sirens unless conditions change). Notify dispatch when you are en route and when you arrive.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Priority 3 — Routine response">
    Non-urgent calls where no immediate danger is present. Examples include minor collisions, report-taking, or welfare checks with no immediate concern.

    **Response code:** Code 1 (normal driving, no lights or sirens). Proceed to the location and notify dispatch on arrival.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Scene management

<Steps>
  <Step title="Announce arrival">
    Notify dispatch when you arrive on scene.

    > *"Niagara dispatch, [Callsign], on scene at [location]."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assess and secure">
    Before engaging with individuals on scene, conduct a quick assessment. Identify hazards, the number of persons involved, and whether additional units are required.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request backup if needed">
    If the scene exceeds your safe capacity to manage alone, request backup immediately — before engaging further.

    > *"Niagara dispatch, [Callsign], requesting backup at [location]. [Brief description of situation]."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take control of the scene">
    Direct persons on scene clearly and calmly. One officer speaks at a time. If a second unit is present, the primary unit manages communication with civilians while the secondary provides cover or perimeter.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clear the scene">
    When the situation is resolved, notify dispatch and confirm disposition.

    > *"Niagara dispatch, [Callsign], clear at [location]. [Disposition — e.g., one arrested, report to follow, unfounded]."*
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Safety and professional conduct

Your conduct during patrol reflects on the entire detachment. Maintain the following standards at all times.

- **Stay in character** — Speak and act as an OPP officer would. Avoid out-of-character commentary on the radio or in proximity to civilians unless there is a technical emergency.
- **Use measured force** — Escalate only as the situation demands. Unnecessary or excessive force undermines realism and may result in a conduct review.
- **Respect the chain of command** — If a supervisor or senior officer is on scene, defer to their direction unless they are giving an instruction that clearly violates SOP.
- **Avoid erratic driving** — Drive realistically unless under a Code 3 response. Reckless driving outside of emergency situations is a conduct issue.
- **Stay aware of your surroundings** — Announce your position and intentions to dispatch and your partner. No one should be surprised by your location or actions.

<Tip>
If you are new to patrol, ask a senior member or your FTO to shadow your first few solo shifts. It is far easier to build good habits early than to unlearn poor ones later.
</Tip>

## Ending a patrol

Sign off properly at the end of every shift. Going offline without notifying dispatch leaves a gap in the duty log and may cause other members to attempt to reach you.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Clear any outstanding calls or reports">
    Before signing off, ensure all active calls are resolved or handed off to another unit. Do not go 10-7 while assigned to an unresolved call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="File pending reports">
    Submit any reports that arose during your shift before or immediately after signing off. Reports should not remain outstanding for more than 24 hours.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notify dispatch">
    Transmit your sign-off to dispatch with your location.

    > *"Niagara dispatch, [Callsign] is 10-7 at [location]. End of shift."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disconnect from duty channel">
    Leave the duty voice channel in Discord once dispatch has acknowledged your sign-off.
  </Step>
</Steps>
