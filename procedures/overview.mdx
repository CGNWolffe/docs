Standard operating procedures (SOPs) define how every member of the OPP GLJRP Niagara Detachment conducts themselves during roleplay. They are the foundation of consistent, professional, and realistic policing in the community — and following them is what separates a credible detachment from an uncoordinated one.

## What are SOPs?

SOPs are documented, agreed-upon methods for handling common situations. Rather than leaving individual members to improvise, SOPs ensure that every patrol, traffic stop, radio transmission, and incident report is handled the same way — regardless of who is on duty. This consistency is what makes the Niagara Detachment's roleplay believable and enjoyable for everyone involved.

<Info>
SOPs apply to all active members during patrols and any official detachment activity. Probationary members under FTO supervision are held to the same standards, with guidance from their training officer.
</Info>

## Why SOPs matter

Professionalism in roleplay is earned through preparation and discipline, not just equipment or rank. SOPs give you a clear framework to fall back on under pressure, reduce uncertainty in complex situations, and ensure that other members can predict your actions and coordinate with you effectively.

When every member follows the same procedures, the detachment operates as a unit — communications are cleaner, scenes are managed more efficiently, and the overall quality of roleplay improves for members and civilians alike.

<Tip>
If you are ever unsure how to handle a situation, default to the relevant SOP. It is always better to follow procedure than to improvise in a way that could undermine realism or cause confusion.
</Tip>

## Core principles

Every SOP in this detachment is built on three principles. Keep these in mind whenever you are on duty.

**Consistency** — Handle the same situation the same way every time. Predictability builds trust with other members and makes coordinated responses possible.

**Professionalism** — Conduct yourself as a real OPP officer would. Your language, actions, and decisions should reflect the standards of the provincial police, even in a roleplay context.

**Realism** — Procedures are based on real OPP and Canadian law enforcement practices. The closer your roleplay reflects reality, the more immersive and credible the experience is for everyone.

<Warning>
Failure to follow SOPs may result in a formal review, additional FTO supervision, or removal from active duty status. Repeated disregard for procedures will be escalated to detachment command.
</Warning>

## Keeping procedures current

SOPs are reviewed and updated periodically by detachment command. When a procedure changes, an announcement is posted in the Discord server. It is your responsibility to read and apply any updates before your next patrol.

If you believe a procedure is outdated or unclear, raise it through the appropriate Discord channel — do not simply ignore or improvise around it.
