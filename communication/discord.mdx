The OPP GLJRP Niagara Discord server is the operational hub for the detachment. Everything outside of in-game patrol — patrol logs, coordination, training, announcements, and feedback — runs through Discord. Get set up and familiar with the server before your first shift.

## Joining the server

<Steps>
  <Step title="Receive your invitation link">
    An invitation link is provided after your application is accepted. Check the confirmation message from command staff or your assigned FTO. Do not share the link publicly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Join with your Discord account">
    Click the invite link and select **Join Server**. If you don't have a Discord account, create one at [discord.com](https://discord.com) first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the rules channel">
    You'll land in a restricted state until you acknowledge the server rules. Read **#rules** carefully and react with the acknowledgement emoji to unlock full member access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your server nickname">
    Update your server nickname to match your in-game display name and callsign. The standard format is:

    ```
    [Rank] [Last Name] | Unit [Number]
    ```

    Example: `Cst. Mackenzie | Unit 42`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive your roles">
    A staff member or bot will assign your rank role after verifying your membership. If your roles are missing after 24 hours, contact a supervisor in **#help-desk**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your Discord roles control which channels you can see and post in. Probationary members have access to a subset of channels until they complete FTO training.
</Note>

## Server rules

Follow these rules at all times. Violations are handled by staff and may result in removal from the server and the detachment.

1. **Be respectful.** No harassment, hate speech, or targeted hostility toward any member.
2. **Use channels for their intended purpose.** Off-topic content in operational channels will be removed.
3. **No sharing of the server invite link** without authorization from command staff.
4. **Keep patrol-log entries accurate and timely.** Do not fabricate or exaggerate patrol activity.
5. **No impersonation.** Do not present yourself as a rank or role you do not hold, in Discord or in-game.
6. **Follow the chain of command.** If you have a concern, raise it through the appropriate channel — supervisor DM, **#help-desk**, or **#suggestions** — not in public channels.
7. **No recording or sharing of private DMs or staff channels** without explicit permission.
8. **English only in operational channels.** This ensures all members can follow communications.

<Warning>
  Command staff reserve the right to remove any member from the server for conduct unbecoming of an OPP GLJRP officer, even if the specific behavior is not listed above.
</Warning>

## Using Discord during patrol

### Logging your patrol

Every patrol — supervised or independent — must be logged in **#patrol-log**. Post your log entry when you go 10-8 and again when you go 10-7.

**Start-of-patrol format:**
```
ON DUTY — Unit [number] | [Rank] [Last Name]
Start time: [HH:MM EST]
Area: [patrol zone or highway]
Status: 10-8
```

**End-of-patrol format:**
```
OFF DUTY — Unit [number] | [Rank] [Last Name]
End time: [HH:MM EST]
Duration: [X hours Y minutes]
Calls attended: [number]
Notes: [brief summary or "no incidents"]
```

<Tip>
  Keep your patrol log accurate. It is reviewed by supervisors and counts toward your activity record for promotions and special assignments.
</Tip>

### Coordinating with other units

Use **#radio-comms** for text-based coordination when:
- You're unable to use voice channels
- You need to share a plate number, address, or name clearly
- The game server's voice chat is experiencing issues

During active incidents, keep **#radio-comms** entries brief and use the same ten-code format as voice radio.

### Voice channels

The server has designated voice channels for patrol operations. Join the appropriate channel for your shift:

- **Patrol — Channel 1 / Channel 2** — General patrol voice. Multiple channels reduce congestion on busy nights.
- **Command / Supervisors** — Sergeant and above. Members are not invited unless called.
- **Training** — FTO sessions. Your FTO will move you here for supervised patrol.
- **AFK** — If you're temporarily unavailable during a patrol session.

## Submitting reports and feedback

### Incident reports

After any significant call — arrests, pursuits, use of force, Signal 2 or above — submit an incident report in **#incident-reports**. Use the pinned template in that channel. Reports should be submitted within 24 hours of the incident.

### Feedback and suggestions

Use **#suggestions** for structured feedback on detachment procedures, Discord organization, or documentation. Follow the pinned template:

1. **Summary** — one sentence describing the suggestion
2. **Rationale** — why this change would benefit the detachment
3. **Proposed change** — specific and actionable

Feedback posted outside of **#suggestions** (such as in **#general**) will not be formally reviewed.

### After-action comments

If you have feedback about a specific patrol session, incident, or interaction with another member, send a direct message to your Sergeant. This keeps discussions private and ensures the right person reviews the information.

<Info>
  The detachment values constructive feedback. Staff review **#suggestions** regularly and respond to all formal submissions.
</Info>
