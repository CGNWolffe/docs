Your callsign is your identity on the radio. It tells dispatch and other units who you are, what rank you hold, and which unit you belong to — all without saying your name. Use this page to understand how the callsign system works and how to use yours correctly.

## What is a callsign?

A callsign is a short alphanumeric identifier assigned to every active member of the detachment. It replaces your name during all radio and in-game communications, keeping transmissions professional and consistent.

Every callsign in OPP GLJRP Niagara follows this structure:

```
[Rank Prefix] - [Unit Number]
```

For example: **Constable 42**, shortened on air to **"Unit 42"** or simply **"42"** in fast exchanges with dispatch.

<Info>
  Supervisory ranks may use a slightly different format — see the rank-based callsign table below.
</Info>

## Callsign structure

### Rank prefixes

Your rank prefix identifies your role within the detachment. It is used when introducing yourself at the start of a shift or when a supervisor needs to distinguish rank quickly.

| Rank | Prefix used on air |
|------|--------------------|
| Constable (Cadet / Probationary) | "Unit [number]" |
| Constable (Full) | "Unit [number]" |
| Senior Constable | "Senior [number]" |
| Corporal | "Corporal [number]" |
| Sergeant | "Sergeant [number]" or "Sgt [number]" |
| Staff Sergeant | "Staff [number]" |
| Inspector | "Inspector" or as directed |
| Dispatch | "Dispatch" |
| Field Training Officer | "FTO [number]" (during supervised patrol) |

### Unit numbers

Unit numbers are assigned sequentially by command staff. They are permanent — your number stays with you for the duration of your membership. Numbers are not reused until a member has been inactive for an extended period as defined by command.

<Note>
  Unit numbers below 10 are reserved for command staff and dispatch supervisors. Standard member numbers begin at 10.
</Note>

## How you receive your callsign

Your callsign is assigned during the onboarding process after you complete your application and are accepted into the detachment.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete your application">
    Submit your application through the detachment's application channel on Discord. Once reviewed and accepted, you'll receive a welcome message from command staff.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attend your intake briefing">
    Command staff or your assigned FTO will conduct a brief intake to confirm your in-game details and rank. Your unit number is assigned at this stage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive your callsign in Discord">
    Your callsign is posted in your personnel thread or the intake channel. You'll also receive your Discord role, which reflects your rank and unit number.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and begin using it">
    Acknowledge your callsign to your FTO or supervisor and begin using it immediately on any supervised or independent patrols.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using your callsign on the radio

### When to state your callsign

You identify yourself with your callsign in these situations:

- At the start of your shift when going 10-8: *"Dispatch, Unit 42, 10-8."*
- When calling dispatch or another unit: *"Dispatch, Unit 42..."*
- When responding to dispatch: Your callsign has already been stated by dispatch, so respond with the code directly after acknowledgement — *"Unit 42, go ahead." → "10-4, en route."*
- At end of shift when going 10-7: *"Dispatch, Unit 42, 10-7."*

### Correct format on air

Follow this pattern when keying your radio:

```
"[Station you are calling], [your callsign]."
```

Then wait for acknowledgement before continuing.

**Example — starting a shift:**
> *"Dispatch, Unit 42, 10-8, patrolling Highway 20 corridor."*

**Example — calling another unit:**
> *"Unit 15, Unit 42, what's your 10-20?"*

**Example — responding to a dispatched call:**
> *[Dispatch: "Unit 42, Signal 1 at 4842 Dorchester Road."]*
> *"Unit 42, 10-4, 10-23 in approximately five."*

<Tip>
  Always say your callsign clearly and without rushing. If dispatch mishears your unit number, it creates confusion and delays response.
</Tip>

## Callsign format examples by rank

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Constable / Probationary Constable">
    **Format:** Unit [number]

    - Unit 42 — *"Dispatch, Unit 42, 10-8."*
    - Unit 17 — *"Unit 17, 10-23."*

    During FTO-supervised patrol, your FTO may introduce both callsigns: *"Dispatch, FTO 8 and Unit 42, 10-8, beginning supervised patrol."*
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Senior Constable">
    **Format:** Senior [number] or Unit [number] depending on context

    Senior Constables use their unit number in routine traffic and may use the "Senior" prefix when the distinction matters — for example, when acting as a scene supervisor.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Corporal">
    **Format:** Corporal [number]

    - *"Dispatch, Corporal 5, 10-8."*

    Corporals are typically shift supervisors. Their callsign signals to all units that a supervisor is active on the channel.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sergeant / Staff Sergeant">
    **Format:** Sergeant [number] or Staff [number]

    - *"Dispatch, Sergeant 3, available."*

    Sergeants and above may also communicate using their position title rather than unit number in formal transmissions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Field Training Officer (FTO)">
    **Format:** FTO [number] during supervised patrol sessions

    When escorting a probationary constable, the FTO identifies both themselves and their trainee at the start of the session. Once the session ends, both units return to their standard callsigns.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Callsign conflicts and changes

### If your callsign conflicts with another member

A conflict occurs when two active members share the same unit number — typically the result of a data entry error during onboarding.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Do not use the conflicting callsign on air">
    Avoid creating confusion by staying off the radio with that callsign until the conflict is resolved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact a supervisor or command staff immediately">
    Send a direct message to a Sergeant or above, or post in the appropriate staff contact channel on Discord. Include both your name and the name of the other member if known.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive a temporary callsign if needed">
    Command staff may issue you a temporary callsign to use while the conflict is sorted out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your permanent assignment">
    Once command resolves the conflict, you'll receive confirmation of your permanent callsign. Update your Discord nickname to reflect it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Requesting a callsign change

Callsign changes are not routine, but command staff will consider requests in specific circumstances — for example, if you have been promoted or if your unit number was assigned in error.

To request a change, send a formal message to command staff via Discord outlining your reason. Do not change your callsign without written confirmation from a Sergeant or above.

<Warning>
  Using a callsign that has not been officially assigned to you — including impersonating a higher rank prefix — is a serious breach of detachment conduct standards and may result in disciplinary action.
</Warning>
